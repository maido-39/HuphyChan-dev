# Rapid Locomotion via Reinforcement Learning
**저자**: Gabriel B. Margolis, Ge Yang, Kartik Paigwar, Tao Chen, Pulkit Agrawal (MIT Improbable AI Lab / NSF AI Institute IAIFI) · **발표**: Robotics: Science and Systems (RSS) 2022; extended in IJRR 2024 · [📄 원문](https://www.roboticsproceedings.org/rss18/p022.pdf)

> **한 줄**: An end-to-end RL controller drives the MIT Mini Cheetah quadruped to a record 3.9 m/s by replacing manual velocity-command curricula with a reward-gated "Grid Adaptive" curriculum that grows the (forward-velocity, yaw-rate) command grid only into cells the policy can already track, plus teacher-student online system ID for sim2real.

## 문제 (왜 필요한가)
Training a single velocity-conditioned RL locomotion policy over a wide command range collapses: if you sample commands uniformly from a large range (e.g. vx in [-4,4] m/s, wz in [-5,5] rad/s) from scratch, most commands are infeasible/high-difficulty, the agent gathers almost no reward, and learning fails (the no-curriculum baseline just jitters in place, tracking error equals the command). Manual command curricula require hand-ranking which (linear, angular) velocity combinations are physically feasible and re-tuning whenever the env or algorithm changes. The paper's core problem: how to automatically expand the command range to high speed without PPO collapse, while respecting the coupling between linear and angular speed (centrifugal force makes simultaneous fast running + turning much harder than either alone).

## 방법
Manager-style velocity-tracking RL in Isaac Gym. Policy maps observation history -> 12 joint position targets fed to a low-gain PD controller (Kp=20, Kd=0.5; chosen deliberately low to promote smooth motion, never tuned). Control at 50 Hz. Observation o_t = [joint angles q (12), joint velocities qdot (12), gravity vector in body frame g_ori (3), previous action a (12)] = 39 dims, plus the 3-dim velocity command (vx, vy, wz) -> 42-dim input x_t; the net consumes a history of H=15 frames. CORE CONTRIBUTION = Grid Adaptive command curriculum: represent the joint distribution over (vx_cmd, wz_cmd) as a discrete 2D grid with resolution [0.5 m/s, 0.5 rad/s] centered at [0,0], initialized uniform over vx in [-1,1] m/s and wz in [-1,1] rad/s (lateral vy_cmd sampled separately from a small fixed uniform range). After each episode with command [vx,wz] earning per-axis tracking rewards r_vx, r_wz: if BOTH r_vx>=gamma AND r_wz>=gamma (gamma is a fixed success threshold in (0,1)), add probability mass to the 4-connected grid neighbors (vx +/-0.5, wz +/-0.5) not yet active; otherwise leave the grid unchanged. The grid thus grows outward only along the boundary of mastered commands, and because it models the JOINT distribution it naturally stops expanding into infeasible high-vx+high-wz corners (vs the weaker "Box Adaptive" variant that grows vx and wz marginals independently and keeps proposing infeasible combos). Sim2real via teacher-student (RMA-style): a teacher with a privileged encoder g(d_t) over true dynamics params d_t is trained with PPO; a student adaptation module h(x[t-h:t-1]) regresses the teacher's latent z (8-dim) from a 15-step state history via supervised loss, trained on-policy SIMULTANEOUSLY with the teacher (their simplification vs original RMA). Domain randomization over friction, restitution, payload mass, CoM, motor strength. Trained on FLAT ground only; still transfers to grass/gravel/ice.

## 핵심 결과
- Record 3.9 m/s top sustained speed on flat ground (Mini Cheetah), beating the prior model-based record of 3.7 m/s; mean 3.8 m/s across seeds when commanded to 6.0 m/s.
- Outdoor 10 m grass dash in 2.94 s = 3.4 m/s average; max yaw rate 5.7 rad/s (90% of the 6.28 rad/s model-based record, but with a SINGLE policy for linear + angular vs two separate model-based controllers).
- Grid Adaptive curriculum achieves a strictly larger 'command area' (range of jointly-trackable speeds under an error threshold) than Box Adaptive at every error threshold; No Curriculum fails entirely.
- Online system ID closes the sim2real gap at high speed: at commanded 6.0 m/s, teacher/student reached 5.46 m/s sim -> 3.81 m/s real, vs plain domain-randomization 5.07 m/s sim -> 2.49 m/s real (student nearly matches privileged teacher).
- Froude number 5.1 -- first RL system shown to exceed Fr>=1 on this class (concurrently with Ji et al.).
- Rough-terrain training is NOT required for outdoor robustness, but adding training-terrain roughness shrinks flat-ground command area: explicit speed-vs-robustness tradeoff (Fig 5).
- Emergent behaviors with no explicit reward: recovery from tripping/flipping, landing on feet, gait change after a barrier, compensation for a blocked/malfunctioning motor.

## 핵심 수치 (재사용용)
- Robot: MIT Mini Cheetah, 9 kg, 30 cm tall, 12 quasi-direct-drive actuators, max torque 17 N*m
- PD gains: Kp=20, Kd=0.5 (low, untuned, for smoothness); control 50 Hz on Jetson TX2 NX
- Command grid resolution [0.5 m/s, 0.5 rad/s], centered at [0,0]; init uniform vx in [-1,1] m/s, wz in [-1,1] rad/s; gamma = fixed success threshold in (0,1)
- Failure case: uniform sampling over vx in [-4,4] m/s, wz in [-5,5] rad/s collapses training
- Tracking error per cell computed from 5 trials/cell; command-area inverse boundary wz ~ 1/vx
- Domain randomization (Table I): ground friction 0.05-4.00; restitution 0.00-1.00; payload mass -1.0 to +3.0 kg; CoM +/-0.10 m; motor strength 90-110%
- Reward weights (Table VI): xy-vel tracking exp{-|v_xy - v_cmd|^2/sigma} weight +0.02; yaw-vel tracking +0.01; z-velocity -0.04; roll-pitch ang-vel -0.001; base height (h-h0)^2 -0.6; base orientation |g_ori_xy|^2 -0.002; self-collision -0.02; joint-limit violation -0.2; joint torques |tau|^2 -2e-7; joint accelerations |qddot|^2 -5e-9; action rate |a_t-1 - a_t|^2 -2e-4; foot airtime bonus +0.02
- Latent z dim = 8; encoder MLP [256,128]; adaptation module input 42x15 history, MLP [256,32]; policy body input (42 + 8) MLP [512,256,128] -> 12 actions; ELU activations; history h=15
- PPO (Table V): discount 0.99, GAE lambda 0.95, 21 steps/rollout, 5 epochs/rollout, 4 minibatches/epoch, entropy 0.01, value-loss coef 1.0, clip 0.2, lr 1e-3, Adam, reward normalization on, 4096 envs/worker (4000 agents), 400M timesteps (~92 real-time days) in <3 h on one RTX 3090

## 우리 프로젝트 관련성
Directly informs decision (a), your velocity-command curriculum to 2.0 m/s. This is THE reference for the grid-adaptive curriculum you already use. Adopt their exact mechanism: a discrete grid over (vx_cmd, wz_cmd) at [0.5 m/s, 0.5 rad/s] resolution, init uniform over [-1,1]x[-1,1], expand a cell's 4-connected neighbors only when BOTH per-axis tracking rewards clear a fixed threshold gamma. Because it models the JOINT distribution it auto-avoids the infeasible high-vx+high-wz corner -- relevant for a biped where simultaneous fast walking + turning is even more dynamically fragile than for a quadruped. Reaching 2.0 m/s is far inside their proven envelope (they hit 3.9 m/s on a 9 kg quad), so the method has ample headroom; your collapse risk is exactly the failure mode (uniform wide-range sampling) they diagnose. For (b) foot vibration / ankle-accel: their reward table is a ready-made template -- they use action-rate |a_t-1 - a_t|^2 at -2e-4, joint accelerations |qddot|^2 at -5e-9, and joint torques |tau|^2 at -2e-7, exactly the smoothness regularizers you want; combined with deliberately LOW PD gains (Kp=20, Kd=0.5) for smooth motion. For (c) keeping the toe passive: their action space is strictly the 12 actuated joints; the curriculum/reward formulation is agnostic to your passive toe spring, so it cleanly supports keeping the toe out of the policy. For (e) sim2real: their DR ranges (motor strength 90-110%, payload -1 to +3 kg, friction 0.05-4.0, CoM +/-0.10 m) plus on-policy teacher-student online system ID are a strong, hardware-light recipe (gyro + joint encoders only) that maps onto your load-measurement platform.

## 한계
Quadruped (Mini Cheetah), not a biped -- bipeds have a much smaller support polygon, stronger lateral-stability and gait-timing constraints, and your passive toe spring + 14 DOF morphology are absent here; expect the feasible command region to be smaller and the wz~1/vx feasibility boundary to bite sooner. Curriculum operates only over (vx, wz); lateral vy_cmd is sampled from a fixed small uniform range, NOT curriculumed -- if you need lateral agility you must extend the grid yourself. No periodic-gait / contact-schedule reward (decision d): their only contact-related term is a simple foot-airtime bonus (+0.02), so this paper does NOT cover your forefoot-loading / phase-clock reward -- look to gait-conditioned policies (e.g. Margolis & Agrawal's 'Walk These Ways', or Siekmann/Cassie periodic rewards) for that. The fixed success threshold gamma is stated as a constant in (0,1) but its exact value is not given -- you must tune it. Trained on flat ground; they show rough-terrain training trades away top speed, so your flat-then-rough plan should expect a speed regression when terrain is added. Blind policy, no vision. 50 Hz control with deliberately low PD gains is tuned for the Mini Cheetah's QDD actuators -- revalidate against your RobStride actuators and the dynamics of the passive toe.

## 우리 적용 (takeaway)
Implement the curriculum exactly as specified: 2D grid over (vx_cmd, wz_cmd) at [0.5 m/s, 0.5 rad/s], init uniform [-1,1]x[-1,1], expand 4-connected neighbors only when both per-axis tracking rewards exceed a tuned gamma; this is your safe path to 2.0 m/s without collapse (well within their envelope). Sweep gamma (start ~0.6-0.8) since the paper omits the value. Port their reward weights as your vibration baseline -- action-rate -2e-4, joint-accel -5e-9, joint-torque -2e-7 -- and apply an EXTRA, higher-weight acceleration/action-rate penalty specifically on the ankle joints to kill foot vibration, since their global weights are tuned for a quad without your ankle/passive-toe coupling. Keep the 12 actuated joints as the action space and leave the toe spring out of the policy, consistent with their design. Use their DR ranges and on-policy teacher-student online system ID as your sim2real starting point. Do NOT expect this paper to give you a forefoot contact-schedule reward -- pair it with a separate gait-conditioned/periodic-reward paper for decision (d).
