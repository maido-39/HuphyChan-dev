# Sim-to-Real Learning of All Common Bipedal Gaits via Periodic Reward Composition
**저자**: Jonah Siekmann, Yesh Godse, Alan Fern, Jonathan Hurst (Oregon State University, CoRIS / Dynamic Robotics Lab) · **발표**: IEEE ICRA 2021 (arXiv:2011.01387v2, Mar 2021) · [📄 원문](https://arxiv.org/abs/2011.01387)

> **한 줄**: A clock-indexed, von-Mises-smoothed reward that penalizes foot FORCE during swing and foot VELOCITY during stance, composed per-leg with phase offsets, learns every common bipedal gait (walk/run/hop/gallop/skip/stand) with PPO and transfers to Cassie hardware without any reference trajectory.

## 문제 (왜 필요한가)
How to SPECIFY a gait through a reward function so RL reliably reproduces it across seeds without (a) reference/motion-capture trajectories that over-constrain the motion or (b) underspecified "move forward" rewards that give huge behavior variance and need endless hand-shaping. The authors want one intuitive, parametric reward family that covers the whole spectrum of periodic bipedal gaits and still transfers sim-to-real.

## 방법
Index time by a cyclic phase phi that wraps over [0,1] each gait cycle (it does NOT increase monotonically). The total reward is R(s,phi)=beta+sum_i c_i * I_i(phi) * q_i(s): a phase coefficient c_i (sign decides reward vs penalty), a phase INDICATOR I_i(phi), and a measured quantity q_i(s) (e.g. norm of a foot force). I_i is a Bernoulli random variable that is 1 when phi falls inside an interval [A_i,B_i]; A_i,B_i are drawn from Von Mises distributions Phi(2*pi*a_i,kappa) and Phi(2*pi*b_i,kappa), so the shared concentration kappa SMOOTHS the swing<->stance boundary (this smoothing is reported as key to stable, consistent learning). They train on the deterministic EXPECTATION E[R] (linearity of expectation makes this equivalent in return). The gait primitive: in SWING penalize foot force and ignore foot velocity (c_swing_frc=-1, c_swing_spd=0); in STANCE penalize foot velocity and ignore force (c_stance_spd=-1, c_stance_frc=0). One ratio r in (0,1) splits the cycle: swing lasts r, stance lasts 1-r. Define C_frc(phi) and C_spd(phi) as the expected sums of these indicator*coefficient products; per-foot reward = E[C_frc]*q_frc + E[C_spd]*q_spd. Going bipedal just adds two cycle-offset parameters theta_left, theta_right that shift each foot's identical phase sequence: |theta_left-theta_right|~0.5 gives walk/run, ~0 gives hop, intermediate shifts give gallop; skipping needs FOUR phases per foot. Auxiliary (aperiodic) costs treat themselves as single-phase components active the whole cycle: command tracking (forward/lateral speed error, orientation), and smoothness/regularization (action difference, joint torque, pelvis acceleration). The LSTM policy gets the clock as sin-encoded inputs p=[sin(2pi(phi+theta_left)/L), sin(2pi(phi+theta_right)/L)] plus the phase ratios r and the speed command, so the MDP stays stationary. Single-gait policies hold r, theta_left, theta_right fixed; multi-gait policies vary r (and add transition/hop-symmetry/standing penalties) to morph between gaits and stand.

## 핵심 결과
- Trains standing, walking, running, hopping, galloping, and skipping from the SAME reward family, each just by setting the ratio r and offsets theta_left/theta_right; single gaits described as straightforward to learn across seeds.
- Demonstrated sim-to-real transfer to the Cassie biped (cassie-mujoco-sim / MuJoCo -> hardware) with dynamics randomization only (no system ID, no reference traj).
- A single multi-gait LSTM policy continuously transitions between all 2-beat gaits AND standing while moving (e.g. hopping->galloping->walking on the fly).
- Outdoor robustness on a blind robot: hopping on/off a sidewalk, walking with one foot on a curb, running over small bumps, descending 5 stairs in a running gait, turning on turf -- with feet observed slipping off small ledges and staying compliant to ground slope, indicating the policy prioritizes a FORCE profile over exact foot position.
- Validation that the reward shapes contact: measured simulation GRFs track the commanded E[C_frc] -- near -1 the policy withholds foot force (swing), near 0 it applies force (stance).

## 핵심 수치 (재사용용)
- Phase coefficients: c_swing_frc=-1, c_swing_spd=0, c_stance_spd=-1, c_stance_frc=0; auxiliary command/smoothness costs use c=-1 over the whole cycle.
- Gait offsets: |theta_left-theta_right| ~= 0.5 -> walk/run; ~= 0 -> hop; intermediate -> gallop; skipping needs 4 phases/foot.
- Multi-gait reward weights: 0.400*R_bipedal + 0.300*R_cmd + 0.100*R_smooth + 0.100*(omega-1)*q_standing + 0.100*(-1)*q_hop_sym + 1 (constant bias).
- Reward kernels (all 1-exp(-k*err), bounded [0,1]): q_frc=1-exp(-omega*||foot_frc||^2/100); q_spd=1-exp(-2*omega*||foot_spd||^2); q_xdot/q_ydot=1-exp(-2*omega*|v_des-v_act|); q_orientation=1-exp(-3*(1-(quat_act . quat_des)^2)); q_action_diff=1-exp(-5*||a_t - a_{t-1}||); q_torque=1-exp(-0.05*||tau||); q_pelvis_acc=1-exp(-0.10*(||pelvis_rot||+||pelvis_acc||)).
- Standing gate: omega=(1+exp(-50*(r_swing-0.15)))^-1 (~1 when swing ratio in [0.35,0.7], ~0 near zero swing); normal-locomotion swing/stance ratios live in [0.35,0.7].
- Network: LSTM, 2 recurrent hidden layers of 128 each, linear output of size 30 = 10 desired joint positions + 10 PD-gain deltas; outputs added to neutral standing offsets and neutral PD gains (zeros -> stand).
- Rates: policy at 40 Hz, on-robot PD controllers at 2000 Hz.
- PPO: batch 32 trajectories x up to 300 timesteps, LR 1e-4 (actor and critic), replay buffer 50,000 samples, 4 epochs/iter, 150,000,000 samples total, 24-36 h/policy; fixed exploration action noise std = e^-1 (~0.368); recurrent critic mirrors actor; mirror-loss for symmetry.
- Dynamics randomization (uniform, fixed per rollout): joint damping [0.3,4.0]x default; joint mass [0.5,1.5]x default; ground friction [0.35,1.1]; ground slope [-0.03,0.03] rad; joint encoder offset [-0.05,0.05] rad; plus randomized policy execution rate.
- Policy inputs: estimated pelvis orientation + rotational velocity + joint positions/velocities, desired xdot/ydot, phase ratios r, and clock p=[sin(2pi(phi+theta_left)/L), sin(2pi(phi+theta_right)/L)].

## 우리 프로젝트 관련성
This is the canonical source for decision (d), the contact-schedule / periodic-gait reward, and it maps almost 1:1 onto our 12-actuated-DOF biped with passive forefoot. The force-in-swing / velocity-in-stance pair is exactly the lever to LOAD our passive toe spring (k=60 N*m/rad): during stance the policy must keep foot velocity near zero while the toe deflects under GRF, and our Isaac foot-GRF / link-reaction-wrench sensing (per MEMORY) is precisely the q_frc signal this reward consumes. It directly supports decision (c): the toe stays OUT of the action space -- the reward shapes contact via force/velocity measured at the foot, not via a commanded toe joint, so a passive spring loads naturally. For decision (a), the curriculum to 2.0 m/s: their multi-gait insight is that varying ratio r continuously lets a walk grow into a run, and that varying offsets AND ratios together is the hard case that collapses into asymmetric/fused gaits -- so for us, ramp the SPEED command (and r) while holding offsets fixed, and add transition penalties to keep the gait from degenerating. For decision (b), foot vibration: they regularize with q_action_diff (action smoothness, weight inside R_smooth) and q_pelvis_acc, plus letting the policy output PD-gain deltas; the von-Mises kappa smoothing of phase boundaries is an extra anti-chatter mechanism we can copy at the swing/stance transition. For decision (e), their entire sim2real recipe is dynamics randomization with the concrete ranges in Table I, transferred to Cassie with no system ID -- a directly reusable starting envelope for Isaac Lab.

## 왜 읽나 (한 줄)
**It gives you a drop-in, fully-specified reward (exact coefficients, kernels, and weights) that loads a passive forefoot by construction and covers walk->run on one policy -- the precise shaping our velocity env and GRF sensing were built to exploit.**

## 한계
No quantitative speeds, no tracking-error tables, no ablations with numbers -- results are qualitative (videos + GRF-vs-C_frc plot), so you cannot cite a top speed or a curriculum schedule from this paper (the 1.5/2.0/2.5 m/s figures floating around generic Cassie write-ups are NOT in this paper -- do not quote them). Cassie has springy series-elastic legs and a different morphology from our rigid 51.8 kg / 0.87 m biped, so the damping/mass multipliers and the e^-1 noise are starting points, not transfer-guaranteed values. Control runs at only 40 Hz policy / 2000 Hz PD; Isaac Lab manager-based envs commonly run the policy faster, so action-diff weights and kappa may need retuning. The framework needs the clock phase fed to the policy and the reward indexed by phi -- our rsl_rl PPO + manager-based velocity env will need a custom phase/clock term and a contact reward term (not stock). Multi-gait training is explicitly fragile (asymmetric-walk-instead-of-hop failure mode) and required hand-added transition penalties. It is on-policy PPO with a recurrent (LSTM) actor-critic and mirror loss; our stock rsl_rl is typically MLP -- adopting the reward is easy, adopting the exact policy class is more work. Their PD gains are LEARNED deltas over a neutral set (neutral values not published), so you cannot lift specific PD numbers for our RobStride actuators.

## 우리 적용 (takeaway)
Adopt the periodic contact reward for our biped: add a cyclic term that (1) penalizes per-foot GRF norm during swing and (2) penalizes per-foot linear velocity norm during stance, gated by a von-Mises-smoothed phase indicator with the swing/stance ratio r, and feed sin/cos of the per-foot phase (phi+theta) plus r into the observation. Keep offsets at |theta_L-theta_R|=0.5 for a symmetric walk first; load the passive toe purely through the stance force term (toe stays out of actions/obs, deflecting under the rewarded GRF). Reuse the bounded 1-exp(-k*err) kernels and the regularizers verbatim as a starting point -- q_action_diff (k=5) for action smoothness and q_pelvis_acc / a base-acc analog for vibration, with weights in the published 0.4/0.3/0.1/0.1 ballpark -- to attack the foot-vibration issue (b). For the 2.0 m/s curriculum (a), ramp the speed command (and optionally r) while holding offsets fixed to avoid the fused-gait collapse they warn about, adding a transition/symmetry penalty if the gait degenerates. Seed Isaac Lab domain randomization with Table I ranges (damping 0.3-4.0x, mass 0.5-1.5x, friction 0.35-1.1, slope +-0.03 rad, encoder offset +-0.05 rad, randomized control rate), then widen for sim2real (e). Do NOT cite any specific top-speed number from this paper -- it reports none.
