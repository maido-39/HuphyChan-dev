## A. 골반 ROM (3 plane) — Human Pelvis review (Lewis/Laudicina/Khuu/Loverro 2017, J Sport Phys Ther / PMC5545133)
URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC5545133/  (n=44 healthy adults, preferred speed)

- **Pelvic TILT (sagittal, ant/post)**: "approximately **2 to 5 degrees**" total excursion. Study data mean **4.3° (SD 1.1, range 2.6–7.3)**. Timing: "Following initial contact, the pelvis tilts posteriorly for less than 20% of the gait cycle." (≈2 cycles/stride — biphasic)
- **Pelvic OBLIQUITY (frontal, list/drop)**: "approximately **6 to 11 degrees** at preferred walking speed." Study mean **7.4° (SD 2.5, range 1.9–12.5)**. Timing: neutral at IC, drops toward swing side for <20% GC. (1 drop+return per step → 2/stride)
- **Pelvic ROTATION (transverse)**: "as low as **3 degrees** ... as high as **14 degrees**." Study mean **9.5° (SD 2.9, range 4.0–16.8)**. "one cycle of motion ... throughout each gait cycle" (1/stride).
- **Sex**: females +1.9° obliquity (p<0.001), +2° rotation (p=0.029), more anterior tilt.

## B. Vertical CoM displacement amplitude + M-shape
- **Saunders/Inman/Eberhart canon + modern**: vertical CoM total excursion ≈ **~3–5 cm** at preferred speed; classic textbook figure **~5 cm (2 inch)** total peak-to-peak.
  - Gard et al. (J Biomech Eng 1998, ASME) / vertical-displacement methods studies: "**nearly constant vertical displacement of 3.0 cm (SD 0.4)**."
    URL: https://asmedigitalcollection.asme.org/biomechanical/article/120/1/133/
  - Orendurff et al. 2004 (effect of speed): vertical CoM excursion **2.74 ± 0.52 cm (slow)** → **4.83 ± 0.92 cm (fast)**. URL: https://pubmed.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/15685471/
- **M-shape / two peaks per stride**: CoM rises to **two maxima per stride at mid-single-support (the "vault"/inverted-pendulum apex, CoM highest)** and falls to **two minima at double-support (CoM lowest)**. Potential energy peaks ~midstance, kinetic energy peaks at the step-to-step transition (out of phase = pendular exchange).
- **3D path "bow-tie"**: total path ~**18 cm/stride** (after subtracting forward travel). Mediolateral excursion **~5 cm at 1.4 m/s** (up to ~10 cm at 0.3 m/s). Source: Frontiers Neurol 2019 review, https://www.frontiersin.org/journals/neurology/articles/10.3389/fneur.2019.00999/full

## C. Six Determinants of Gait — the rises/falls are FUNCTIONAL, not noise
URL: https://whatispodiatry.com/the-six-determinants-of-gait-foundations-of-efficient-human-locomotion/ , https://podiapaedia.org/wiki/biomechanics/gait/determinants-of-gait/
- Pelvic ROTATION ~**8° total** (4° fwd swing side / 4° back stance) → *lowers* CoG vertical excursion by **~9.5 mm**.
- Pelvic TILT/obliquity drop ~**5°** contralateral at midstance → lowers CoG rise by **~3 mm**.
- Stance KNEE FLEXION ~**5°** at midstance → lowers the midstance peak.
- ANKLE/FOOT rockers (heel rise + plantarflexion) → *smooth* the CoM path & propel.
- These exist to *flatten the M* (reduce energy) — BUT a real human still keeps ~3–5 cm; the pelvis/limbs are not rigid, they trade a controlled rise for pendular energy return.

## D. Minimizing CoM motion COSTS energy (Ortega & Farley, J Appl Physiol 2005)
URL: https://journals.physiology.org/doi/full/10.1152/japplphysiol.00103.2005 (PMID 15890757)
- Subjects walked normally vs "flat-trajectory" walking (knees bent, CoM held flat).
- **Flattening CoM vertical motion INCREASED net metabolic cost by ~6% even though it reduced CoM work**, because of greater muscle force to support the body in crouch and loss of pendular exchange. Headline: *a flat CoM is not free — the natural vault is metabolically optimal.*

## E. Push-off ↔ CoM rise/redirection coupling
- **Adamczyk & Kuo / Kuo step-to-step transition**: trailing-limb ankle **push-off redirects CoM velocity** at double support; CoM energy change during push-off = **86–96% of ankle push-off energy**. Push-off *precedes* the contralateral collision and offsets it.
  URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC2726857/
- **Reduced ankle plantarflexion = expensive** (Huang/Kuo, PMC4664043, ~50% push-off cut via AFO):
  - "up to ~50% greater net metabolic power to walk at same speed."
  - "~2 W extra metabolic demand per 1 W reduction in push-off work."
  - "for each 1 J reduction in ankle work, 0.6 J more dissipative COLLISION work by the other leg."
  - Mechanism: push-off redirects CoM trajectory, reducing collision losses; KNEE compensates with more stance work.
  URL: https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC4664043/
- **Impulsive ankle push-off powers leg swing** (Lipfert et al., JEB 2014): push-off is impulsive in late stance, accelerating CoM up-and-forward into the next vault.
  URL: https://journals.biologists.com/jeb/article/217/8/1218/

## F. Inverted-pendulum vaulting (the why)
- Single support: stiff stance leg, CoM **arcs forward AND upward over the planted foot** (vault apex = CoM highest). PE↔KE exchange reduces muscle work. Late stance: impulsive ankle push-off feeds energy in; early next stance: collision dissipates. (a-z-animals locomotion overview + Kuo dynamic-walking canon.)
